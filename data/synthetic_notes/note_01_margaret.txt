Home Visit Note — Participant ID: HV-001
Date of visit: 03/12

Participant is an 82-year-old woman living alone in a two-story house. She reports difficulty remembering to take her blood pressure and diabetes medications and sometimes doubles up when she forgets. She has experienced two falls in the past six months, both on the stairs leading to the second-floor bedroom. There are loose throw rugs in the hallway. Her daughter visits once a week to help with groceries and mail but works full time and lives 40 minutes away. The participant expressed feeling isolated, especially in the evenings. She uses a cane but does not have grab bars in the bathroom. She was oriented to time and place during the visit but repeated the same question about her next appointment three times. No smoke detector was observed in the kitchen.
